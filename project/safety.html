It is essential that you plan for safety when hiking in Arizona.

Stay Hydrated

Carry at least 1 liter of water per hour of hiking, especially in hotter months. Arizonaâ€™s dry climate can dehydrate you quickly, so drink small sips regularly rather than waiting until you're thirsty.

Start Early or Late

Avoid the midday heat by hiking early in the morning or later in the evening. The cooler parts of the day are safer and more enjoyable, especially during summer.

Wear Sun Protection

Use sunscreen with SPF 30+ and reapply every two hours. Wear a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, and light, breathable clothing that covers your skin.

Watch the Weather

Check the forecast before your hike. Arizona can have sudden changes, and during monsoon season (July to September), thunderstorms and flash floods are possible. Avoid hiking if thereâ€™s a storm forecasted, especially in canyons.

Know the Trail and Your Limits

Choose a trail that matches your fitness and experience level, and research its difficulty, elevation, and estimated time to complete. Overestimating your ability on a challenging trail can lead to dangerous situations.

Use a Map or GPS

Familiarize yourself with the trail map and have a GPS or map app handy. Cell service can be spotty in remote areas, so consider downloading offline maps.

Carry Essentials

Bring a first aid kit, headlamp or flashlight, extra water, snacks, and a charged mobile phone. A pocketknife, whistle, and emergency blanket are also helpful in emergencies.

Pace Yourself

Arizonaâ€™s desert terrain can be rugged, with steep climbs and loose rocks. Take breaks often, pace yourself, and don't hesitate to turn back if youâ€™re tired or feel unwell.

Look Out for Wildlife

Watch for rattlesnakes, scorpions, and other wildlife. Keep a safe distance, stay on marked trails, and avoid putting your hands or feet in places you can't see.

Tell Someone Your Plan

Before you go, tell a friend or family member where you're hiking and when you expect to return. In remote areas, this can be a critical safety measure.
